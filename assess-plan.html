Protect against physical threats

Updated 9 April 2024

Simple explanation

  • Think about inexpensive, low-tech security measures
  • Create a physical security policy, with colleagues and family
  • Have a communication plan in case of emergency
  • What goes in your plan?
  • Create an office access policy
  • When working outside your home or office
  • Consider using surveillance cameras and motion sensors
  • Protect your local network
  • Prevent accidents and outages
  • Protect against theft, tampering and confiscation
  • Consider what can be seen
  • Decide how you will dispose of sensitive information
  • More resources

Simple explanation for audience

  • Start with low-cost improvements: strong locks, cable locks, privacy screens, and visitor logging.
  • Decide who can enter which area, at what times, and who reports incidents.
  • Prepare emergency contacts and a clear call tree before problems happen.
  • Protect routers and local Wi-Fi with strong admin passwords and updated firmware.
  • Prevent data exposure from visible whiteboards, open desks, and poorly disposed papers.
  • Plan secure destruction for papers, storage devices, and old hardware.
Presenter note: emphasize that physical access often defeats digital security. If someone can touch the device, risk rises quickly.

Create and protect multiple online identities

Updated 9 September 2024

Simple explanation

  • Develop your strategy
  • Check what information exists already on you online
  • Get rid of unwanted information and old identities
  • Map your social domains
  • Create and secure an alternative identity
  • Keep your identities separate
  • Compartmentalize each identity using different devices, profiles or virtual environments
  • Use payment methods that will not reveal your official identity
  • Reflect on how to manage your multiple identities in physical spaces
  • [Advanced] Create a website that cannot be connected to your identity

Simple explanation for audience

  • Do not mix all activities in one account profile.
  • Use different emails, usernames, browser profiles, and recovery numbers per identity.
  • Reduce traceable links: profile photos, reused bios, or cross-post timing.
  • Compartmentalize high-risk work with separate devices or virtual machines.
  • For advanced anonymity, avoid personal payments and hosting methods tied to legal identity.
Presenter note: identities fail when operational habits leak. Reused contact info is one of the fastest ways identities get linked.

Protect the privacy of your online communications

Updated 19 June 2025

Simple explanation

  • Map your communications and make a plan to protect them
  • Create multiple identities
  • Protect and anonymize your connections
  • Use email more safely
  • Use messaging apps more safely
  • Take identifying information out of your photos and other files

Simple explanation for audience

  • List who you communicate with, what you share, and what could go wrong.
  • Use encrypted messaging for sensitive conversation and verify contacts.
  • Use email carefully: strong account security, phishing checks, and limited metadata.
  • When needed, anonymize network traffic and remove metadata from shared files.
Presenter note: privacy is not one tool. It is a workflow: safer identity, safer connection, safer content sharing.

Protect your information and devices while traveling

Updated 3 June 2025

Simple explanation

  • Basic security practices
  • Prepare for crossing borders
  • Use encryption to protect your data and communications
  • Public access
  • Internet censorship and blockages
  • Mobile phone security
  • References

Simple explanation for audience

  • Travel with minimum data and only essential devices.
  • Expect device checks at borders and prepare a reduced-risk profile.
  • Encrypt storage and keep backups in a safe location before departure.
  • Avoid untrusted charging points and unknown public networks.
  • Know legal and network restrictions in destination countries.
Presenter note: travel security starts before leaving. Most damage happens when preparation is missing.
