Assess & Plan

Think like a defender, not a victim

This chapter helps you spot risk early, make simple protection habits, and avoid common attacks before they start.

Risk First Simple Steps Daily Safety

What Do You Need to Protect?

  • Personal Information (name, phone, passwords)
  • Devices (phone, laptop, USB)
  • Online Accounts (email, social media)
  • Communication (messages, calls)
  • Identity (who you are online)
If these are not protected, attackers can hack, spy, or steal your data.

Assess & Plan

What it means: Think before acting and understand your risks.

Protect Against Physical Threats

What are physical threats? Someone steals or accesses your device physically.

Examples:

  • Phone stolen
  • Someone opens your laptop without permission
  • USB infected with virus

How to protect:

  • Use password or fingerprint lock
  • Do not leave devices unattended
  • Avoid using unknown USBs
  • Keep your devices in safe places

Create and Protect Multiple Online Identities

What it means: Do not use one account for everything.

Example:

  • One email for school or work
  • One email for social media
  • One email for unknown websites

Why it is important: If one account is hacked, others can still stay safe.

How to protect:

  • Use different passwords
  • Do not share personal information everywhere
  • Use minimal or non-sensitive info when possible

Protect Your Online Communication Privacy

What is communication privacy? Keeping your messages and calls private.

Risks:

  • Hackers reading messages
  • Tracking your conversations
  • Data leaks

How to protect:

  • Use secure apps (encrypted messaging)
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive chats
  • Do not share passwords or private information
  • Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)

Protect Devices While Traveling

Why this is important: Travel increases risk of loss, theft, or hacking.

Examples:

  • Using public Wi-Fi at an airport
  • Losing your phone
  • Someone accessing your laptop

How to protect:

  • Back up your data before travel
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi or use a VPN
  • Log out of accounts on shared devices
  • Keep devices with you at all times

Related Tools (Help You Stay Safe)

  • Password Managers: Store strong passwords safely
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network): Protect your internet connection
  • Antivirus Software: Detect and remove viruses
  • Secure Messaging Apps: Protect your chats

Important Tip

Most attacks happen because of weak passwords, carelessness, and lack of awareness.

Best protection: be careful + stay informed + use basic security tools.
