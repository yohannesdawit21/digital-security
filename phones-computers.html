Phones & Computers

Simple guide

Your phones and computers need strong protection

Your devices store personal data, passwords, photos, files, and important accounts. If compromised, attackers can get full access to your digital life.

Protect Against Malware

What is malware? Malicious software that harms or steals your data.

Types of malware:

Virus
Spyware
Ransomware
Trojan

Simple understanding notes:

  • Virus: attaches to files and spreads when you run them.
  • Spyware: silently collects your data like passwords or browsing activity.
  • Ransomware: locks your files and asks for money to unlock them.
  • Trojan: pretends to be useful software but opens a backdoor for attackers.
Watch malware types explained

How malware infects:

  • Downloading cracked apps/software
  • Clicking unknown links
  • Using infected USB devices
  • Opening fake email attachments

How to protect:

  • Install apps only from trusted sources
  • Do not download cracked software
  • Use antivirus software
  • Keep your system updated
  • Avoid suspicious links and files

Protect Your Android Device

Risks:

  • Fake apps
  • Malware from APK files
  • Unsafe permissions

How to stay safe:

  • Download apps only from Google Play Store
  • Avoid APK files from unknown websites
  • Check app permissions carefully
  • Enable screen lock (PIN or fingerprint)
  • Keep Android updated

Protect Your iOS Device

Good news: iPhones are generally more secure, but risks still exist.

Risks:

  • Phishing attacks
  • Unsafe Wi-Fi networks

How to protect:

  • Use strong passcode or Face ID
  • Download apps only from App Store
  • Enable Find My iPhone
  • Keep iOS updated

Protect Your Windows Computer

Common target for hackers.

Risks:

  • Viruses and ransomware
  • Fake software downloads
  • USB infections

How to protect:

  • Install antivirus (Windows Defender is good baseline)
  • Keep Windows updated
  • Avoid cracked software
  • Scan USB before opening
  • Enable firewall

Protect Your Mac Computer

More secure, but not 100% safe.

Risks:

  • Malware disguised as apps
  • Phishing attacks

How to protect:

  • Install apps only from trusted sources
  • Enable Gatekeeper
  • Keep macOS updated
  • Use strong passwords
  • Be careful with downloads

Protect Your Linux Computer

Strong security, but depends on the user.

Risks:

  • Misconfiguration
  • Unsafe downloads
  • Weak permissions

How to protect:

  • Use trusted repositories
  • Keep system updated
  • Use strong passwords
  • Limit root/admin access
  • Use firewall (UFW)

Final Important Tip

Most device attacks happen because of downloading unsafe apps, ignoring updates, and clicking unknown links.

Stay safe by installing carefully, updating regularly, and not trusting unknown sources.
