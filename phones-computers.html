Phones & Computers Overview

Protect against malware

Updated 9 April 2024

Simple explanation

  • Update all software
  • Be aware that malicious actors may try to pressure you to act quickly or appeal to your emotions
  • Pause before you click and be cautious when you receive a link
  • Use caution when opening attachments
  • Make file extensions on your computer visible to avoid being tricked by malware
  • Avoid suspicious pop-up windows
  • Use antivirus or anti-malware
  • Use good hygiene with USB cables and devices
  • Use good hygiene with sensitive information
  • Secure other smart devices
  • Reboot your device often, and switch it off overnight
  • If you suspect your device has been infected...
  • Advanced strategies
  • Avoid unlicensed software and consider free and open-source alternatives
Presenter note: emphasize behavior-based protection first. Most infections start with rushed clicks, untrusted files, or unsafe software sources.

Protect your Android device

Updated 17 September 2025

Simple explanation

  • Use the latest version of your device's operating system (OS)
  • Regularly update all installed apps
  • Use apps from trusted sources
  • Remove apps that you do not need and do not use
  • If possible, avoid using social media apps
  • Use privacy-friendly apps
  • Check your app permissions
  • Turn off location and wipe history
  • Create separate user accounts on your devices
  • Remove unneeded users associated with your device
  • Secure the Google accounts connected with your device
  • Set your screen to sleep and lock
  • Control what can be seen when your device is locked
  • Disable voice controls
  • Use a physical privacy filter that prevents others from seeing your screen
  • Use a camera cover
  • Turn off connectivity you are not using
  • Clear your saved wifi networks
  • Turn off sharing you are not using
  • Make sure your keyboard respects your privacy
  • Advanced: figure out whether someone has accessed your device without your permission (basic forensics)
  • Advanced: Use Android without a Google account
  • Advanced: Change the operating system of your Android device
  • See also
Presenter note: permissions and account security are critical. Even updated phones are risky if app permissions and connected accounts are not controlled.

  • Use privacy-friendly apps

  • Set your screen to sleep and lock
  • Control what can be seen when your device is locked
  • Disable voice controls
  • Use a physical privacy filter that prevents others from seeing your screen
  • Use a camera cover
  • Turn off connectivity you are not using
  • Clear your saved wifi networks
  • Turn off sharing you are not using
  • Make sure you are using the default iOS keyboard
  • Advanced: figure out whether someone has accessed your device without your permission (basic forensics)
  • More resources
Presenter note: Lockdown Mode is important for high-risk users. For general users, updates, account hardening, and permissions are the biggest wins.

  • Turn off location and wipe history
  • Create separate user accounts on your devices

  • Protect your user account and computer with a strong passphrase
  • Set your screen to sleep and lock
  • Control what can be seen when your device is locked
  • Make sure voice controls are not enabled
  • Use a physical privacy filter that prevents others from seeing your screen
  • Use a camera cover
  • Turn off connectivity you are not using
  • Clear your saved wifi networks
  • Turn off sharing you are not using
  • Turn off Autoplay
  • Turn off advertising and other privacy options
  • Protect your device with Windows Security
  • Use a firewall
  • Advanced: Stop malicious code from running
  • Advanced: figure out whether someone has accessed your device without your permission (basic forensics)
Presenter note: for Windows, combine update policy + Microsoft Defender + firewall + reduced app permissions for strongest baseline protection.
