Your Files Overview

Protect your sensitive information

Updated 13 March 2024

Simple explanation

  • Consider deleting old data instead of storing it
  • Consider whether encryption is illegal or suspicious in your jurisdiction
  • If encryption is illegal in your country, consider the following alternatives
  • Consider encrypting your whole device
  • Consider encrypting your external storage devices
  • Consider encrypting just some sensitive files
  • Protect your encrypted folder or volume
  • Use trusted dealers and repair shops
Presenter note: start with the simple message that sensitive data should not be stored longer than needed, and encryption should match your legal and operational risk.

Back up and recover from information loss

Updated 14 March 2024

Simple explanation

  • Recover deleted or lost information
  • Create a backup plan
  • Back up the files in your computer to a local device
  • Back up your phone to a local device
  • Consider whether or not you should back up to cloud services
  • Back up your email
  • Scan and back up printed documents
  • Set a backup schedule
  • Practice recovery
  • Establish procedures for coworkers
  • Other considerations
Presenter note: stress that backups are only useful if recovery is tested. A backup that has never been restored is only a theory.

Destroy sensitive information

Updated 25 March 2024

Simple explanation

  • Clean traces of your work off your device
  • Wipe a whole device
  • Before you sell, give away or dispose of an old computer or phone
Presenter note: explain the difference between deleting a file and securely destroying data. Deletion alone often leaves recoverable traces.

Destroy identifying information

Updated 20 March 2024

Simple explanation

  • Take identifying information out of your photos and other files
  • Remove identifying details from pictures
  • Remove identifying details from videos
  • Disable geolocation in your device
  • Remove metadata from pictures, videos and other files
Presenter note: metadata is often overlooked, but it can reveal where, when, and how a file was created or shared.
