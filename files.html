Your Files

Simple guide

Your files need protection

Your files include documents, photos, videos, personal records, and school or work data. If not protected, they can be stolen, leaked, or lost forever.

Protect Your Sensitive Information

What is sensitive information? Important data you do not want others to see.

Examples:

  • Passwords
  • ID and personal documents
  • Private photos
  • Financial information

Risks:

Data theft
Identity theft
Blackmail or misuse

How to protect:

  • Use strong passwords for files and devices
  • Encrypt important files
  • Do not share sensitive data online
  • Store files in secure locations
  • Lock your device
Think: What happens if someone gets this file?

Back Up and Recover from Information Loss

What is backup? Saving a copy of your files in another place.

Why important:

  • Device can be lost or stolen
  • Virus can delete files
  • System crash

How to backup:

  • Save files to external storage (USB or hard drive)
  • Use cloud storage
  • Back up regularly

Recovery:

  • Restore files from backup
  • Re-download from cloud
No backup means no recovery.

Destroy Sensitive Information

What it means: Completely delete files so no one can recover them.

Deleting normally does not always remove files permanently. They can often still be recovered.

How to destroy properly:

  • Use secure delete tools
  • Overwrite files
  • Factory reset devices before selling

Example: selling laptop without proper wipe means new owner can access old files.

Destroy Identifying Information

What is identifying information? Data that can identify you.

Examples:

  • ID cards
  • Addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Personal documents

How to protect:

  • Do not share unnecessary info
  • Delete old accounts and files
  • Shred physical documents
  • Remove metadata from photos and documents

Example: a shared photo can contain location data that lets someone track you.
