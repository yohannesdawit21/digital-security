How Passwords Are Attacked

1. Brute-force attack

Attackers try many combinations quickly. Short and simple passwords fail fastest.

2. Dictionary attack

Attackers try common words, names, and patterns such as Password123.

3. Credential stuffing

Leaked usernames/passwords from one site are tested on other services.

4. Phishing

Fake login pages trick users into entering real credentials.

5. Keylogging malware

Malicious software records what you type and steals your login details.

6. Shoulder surfing

People nearby watch your screen/keyboard while you sign in.

How to Protect Yourself

Create and maintain strong passwords

Use unique passwords for every account, with 16+ characters when possible.

Manage passwords safely

Use a trusted password manager. Avoid saving passwords in notes or chat.

Use two-factor authentication

Enable app-based 2FA for email, banking, and social media. Keep backup codes offline.
