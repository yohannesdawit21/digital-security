Your Internet Connection

Your internet connection is how you browse websites, use apps, and send messages. If not secure, attackers or authorities can see, track, or block your activity.

How the Internet Works and How It Can Be Censored

How it works (simple):

  • Your device sends a request
  • Internet connects you to a server
  • Server sends data back (website, video, app data)
Like this: You ask, internet delivers.

Examples:

  • Government blocks certain websites
  • School blocks social media
  • ISP tracks your activity
Limited access to information
Monitoring of your activity
Loss of privacy

Browse the Web More Securely

Risks while browsing:

  • Fake websites
  • Tracking cookies
  • Malware downloads
Example: Fake login page looks real and steals your password.

How to stay safe:

  • Check website URL and HTTPS
  • Avoid clicking unknown links
  • Use private or incognito mode when needed
  • Clear cookies regularly
  • Use secure browsers
Always ask: Is this website real?

Anonymize Your Connections and Communications

What is anonymity? Hiding your identity online.

Why it is important:

  • Prevent tracking
  • Protect your identity
  • Avoid surveillance

How to stay anonymous:

  • Use VPN
  • Use anonymous browsers like Tor
  • Avoid sharing personal info
  • Use different accounts and identities
Without protection, websites can see your IP, location, and activity. With anonymity tools, your identity is better hidden.

Stay safe by browsing carefully, using protection tools, and protecting your identity.
